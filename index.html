What is fast fashion?

The growth of fast fashion has been fueled by consumer demand for constant new styles at low prices.

Fast fashion refers to a business model in the clothing industry that focuses on quickly producing and distributing large volumes of inexpensive garments to keep up with rapidly changing trends. Brands using this model often take inspiration from runway designs, social media, and current events, then manufacture similar styles at a lower cost and bring them to market in a short time frame. This approach allows consumers to access trendy clothing at affordable prices, but it also results in frequent product turnover and shorter clothing lifespans. Fast fashion has become a significant part of the global apparel industry, influencing how clothing is designed, produced, and consumed.

Harmful Effects

Carbon Emissions and Energy Use

Fast fashion accounts for approximately 4–10% of global greenhouse gas emissions. Energy-intensive production processes keep demand high, contributing to rising temperatures and ecological disruption worldwide.

Textile Waste and Landfills

Fast fashion's rapid purchase-and-disposal cycle generates massive waste, with about 11 million tons of textiles discarded each year in the United States alone. Much of it ends up in landfills where synthetic fibers can persist for centuries.

Learn more

Microplastics

Synthetic fabrics like polyester, nylon, and acrylic shed tiny plastic fibers during washing. These microplastics often pass through treatment systems and accumulate in rivers and oceans, affecting ecosystems and food chains.
