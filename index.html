The Impact of Fast Fashion

Fast fashion keeps prices low and trends moving, but the true cost is paid by workers, ecosystems, and future generations.

Discover the Story

What is fast fashion?

Fast fashion is a clothing production model focused on quickly turning new trends into low-cost items sold in high volumes.

Harmful effects

This pace can increase textile waste, pollution, and pressure on workers through tight deadlines and low production costs.

Environmental Cost

The fashion industry is one of the world’s largest polluters. Massive water use, chemical dyes, and textile waste all contribute to environmental damage that continues long after clothes are discarded.

Human Impact

Many garment workers face long hours, unsafe conditions, and low wages. Behind each low-cost item is often a labor force with limited protections and few alternatives.

Consumer Power

Choosing quality over quantity, repairing clothes, shopping second-hand, and supporting ethical brands can all reduce the demand that fuels harmful production cycles.
