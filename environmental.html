Carbon Emissions and Energy Use

Fast fashion's environmental toll extends well beyond water. The industry accounts for approximately 4–10% of global greenhouse gas emissions, with carbon output generated at every stage of the supply chain, from raw material extraction to manufacturing, transportation, and retail (Niinimäki et al. 190). Energy-intensive processes like spinning, dyeing, and finishing rely heavily on fossil fuels, and the industry's relentless production pace keeps energy demand and emissions continuously high.

The consequences are already visible in the form of rising temperatures, more frequent extreme weather events, and widespread ecological disruption. Fast fashion's emissions do not exist in isolation; they compound broader environmental pressures and reinforce the urgency of treating the industry as a meaningful contributor to the global climate crisis.
