Take Action

Choosing quality over quantity, repairing clothes, shopping second-hand, and supporting ethical brands can all reduce the demand that fuels harmful production cycles.

Consumer choices shape the market. Small, consistent decisions matter: buying fewer items, extending the life of clothes, and supporting transparent brands can drive meaningful change over time.

1. Buy Smarter

  • Choose quality over quantity and invest in clothing that lasts longer.
  • Support brands that use eco-friendly materials and fair labor practices.
  • Look for signals like organic cotton and recycled fabric options.

2. Care for Your Clothes

  • Wash less often and use cold water to reduce water and energy use.
  • Repair small tears or loose seams instead of throwing items away.
  • Donate, swap, or pass on clothes you no longer wear.

3. Reuse and Recycle

  • Repurpose older clothing into upcycled or DIY projects.
  • Use local textile recycling points for worn-out garments.
  • Sell or give away usable clothes so they stay out of landfills.

4. Spread Awareness

  • Share what you have learned about fast fashion with people around you.
  • Encourage sustainable clothing choices in your school, home, or community.
  • Use social platforms to support slow-fashion habits and campaigns.
