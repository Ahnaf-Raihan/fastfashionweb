Microplastics

Even while in use, fast fashion continues to pollute. Most garments are made from synthetic materials, including polyester, nylon, and acrylic, which shed microscopic plastic fibers during every wash cycle. These microplastics pass through wastewater treatment facilities largely uncaptured and accumulate in rivers, oceans, and marine ecosystems (Bailey et al. 1073).

Once embedded in the environment, they are extraordinarily difficult to remove. Microplastics are ingested by marine organisms and move up the food chain, potentially reaching humans. Their spread underscores a critical point: the environmental impact of fast fashion persists throughout a garment's entire lifecycle, not just at the moment of disposal.
