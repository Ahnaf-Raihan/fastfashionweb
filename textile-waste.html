Textile Waste and Landfills

The environmental burden does not end at production. Fast fashion's cycle of frequent purchasing and rapid disposal generates staggering levels of waste, with the United States alone discarding approximately 11 million tons of textile waste annually (Olivar Aponte et al.). Much of this waste ends up in landfills, where synthetic fibers like polyester can take centuries to decompose, and chemical treatments often prevent even natural fibers from breaking down efficiently.

This is a fundamentally linear system: clothing is produced, briefly used, and discarded with little consideration for what comes after. Textiles sitting in landfills release greenhouse gases and leach contaminants into the surrounding soil, while incineration, often used as an alternative, introduces additional pollutants into the air. The environmental damage of fast fashion does not end with the consumer; it continues accumulating long after garments are thrown away.
